About Us

Our story is one of redemption. In the early days as UVA first-years, we AIM-ed nonsense and laughed about silly little things (mostly laughing AT each other). Somewhere along the road, we became friends, and that friendship became a solid foundation for our relationship. Finally, just before graduation, we knew (admitted) it was more. Dating your close friend was loads of fun – from visiting all the best places to eat in Georgetown to watching movies and eating gelato at Reston Town Center.


We were determined to learn how to grow and share life together. This became more challenging with four years of long distance and several road bumps in our relationship. And yet despite our struggle to be our ideal selves, God met us at all our shortcomings. We find great joy every time we reflect on our journey and see that God keeps blessing us, redeeming our missteps, and sending us off on a precious path together – now as (almost) husband and wife.

From

Esther Kim

I always wondered what kind of man God put on this planet to be with me. I prayed he would be a leader – bold and courageous – and that I would respect him for his strong character. I also shamelessly hoped he would be tall, athletic, and funny! I’m always blown away by how God answers my prayers, and then some.


When we met, Julius had a memorable name, but he would not talk to me. And so I spent the next few years convincing him to be my friend by letting him pick on me. It’s wonderful how this dynamic blossomed into our relationship because I love his company. He is the best kind of friend – one who is fiercely loyal and challenges you with growth. But he is also the sweetest kind of boyfriend – one who earnestly learned how to take care of me in customized ways (like replacing “look tired” in his vocabulary with “seem tired” because I’m so nitpicky about word choice). Julius keeps me grounded beyond what I knew to need, and he was patient enough to let me learn how to need. I am so thankful and excited to spend a lifetime together with my handsome best friend.

From

Julius Seok

Esther and I first met at UVA during our fellowship's lock-in when we had to act out a skit. While I was practicing my peacock dance, she looked at me and asked, "Is that what you are really going to do?". I guess this is when she thought "who is this joofus?" and that is how our relationship began. Over the years, we grew to be good friends and admittedly I had a big crush on her.


It is amazing how easily I can share my thoughts with Esther. I thank God everyday for sending Esther into my life. I adore her company and I appreciate her wanting to know my thoughts, opinions and words. Her faith and love for God has always been an encouragement in our relationship. I can't thank God enough for sending such a fun, loving, encouraging, caring, and supportive wife-to-be to someone like me. I can't wait to spend the rest of my life with the person I love the most.

Open Door Presbyterian Church

3001 Centreville Road, Herndon, VA 20171


Open Door Presbyterian Church is located in Herndon, VA and is easily accessible by multiple highway and local roads. Our ceremony is in the Korean Church building and will be the building to the right when you turn in to the church property.

Top of the Town

1400 N. 14th St. Arlington, VA 22209


Please carpool in groups of 3 or 4 from Herndon to Arlington, as parking spaces at the reception venue are limited. We recommend that our guests take the Dulles Toll Road (Route 267) from ODPC to the Top of the Town in Arlington, VA. However, if you have time to spare, Interstate 66 is another good option for avoiding toll fees. For your convenience, please remember to check for traffic in advance. Directions to the reception venue are located at: http://www.topofthetown.net/directions.asp.


Parking: Continue to the end of N. 14th Street and go left -- Top of the Town's parking lot is about 100 feet up on the right, facing the Prospect House entrance. Remember to enter Top of the Town through the N. 14th Street entrance.
